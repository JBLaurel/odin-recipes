Basic Sourdough Loaf

Description

A simple, rustic loaf, the basic sourdough is great for everything from avocado toast to PB&J. The wet dough lends itself to a cavernous, moist inside while still maintaining a robust and crisp outer crust. The basic sourdough should be every new sourdough-baker's first loaf!

Ingredients

  • 340g (57%) of water
  • 227g (38%) of fed starter
  • 600g of AP flour
  • 15g (2.5%) of salt

Steps

  1. Combine the fed starter and water in a large bowl (or better yet a stand mixer bowl) and mix until the starter is dissolved
  2. Add the flour and lightly mix by hand or stand mixer until combined.
  3. Allow dough to rest for 45 minutes.
  4. Add salt and knead by hand (20-30 minutes) or using your stand mixer's dough hook (8-10 minutes).
  5. allow the dough to rise at room temperature for 24-48 hours, or in the fridge for at least 3 days. The dough will not double in size like a traditional yeast-leavened bread.
  6. 1-2 hours before baking, place a dutch oven in your oven and preheat to 500 degrees.
  7. Once the oven and dutch oven are thoroughly pre-heated, add the loaf to the dutch oven and cover with the lid.
  8. After 15 minutes, reduce the oven to 425 degrees.
  9. After 15 further minutes (30 minutes total), remove the lid from the dutch oven. Continue cooking for an additional 10-20 minutes until the crust is golden brown and the internal temperature of the loaf is 190 degrees.
  10. Rest until cooled, and enjoy!
